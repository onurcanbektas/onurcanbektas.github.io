One the things I love about a truely free market is that it is driven by <b>the people</b> without needing any 3rd party etc., and the collective behaviour of people will determine what will be produced, how much it will be produces, what will be its price, etc., so in a sense people make and control the market, collectively.

One of things I don't like about how the goverment works is that I have to pay taxes which will not be spend on things that I would like them to be. Normally, this is the job of elective officialls, but we know how well that works in practice, so I propose a new taxing system.

Consider the following scheme:

Every citizen, as usual, have to pay  his/her taxes according to the current tax rules, but what the paid taxes will be spent on will be determined by both the goverment and the citizen who paid that that tax. For example, if I pay 100$ taxes this year, a fraction of it, say %60, will go directly to goverment's control, and the rest will will be in my control. When I pay my tax, I will specify what the government should spent that %40 of my taxes; for example, I can say that money should spend on education, on renewable energy industry, on defence, on funding science, etc..

Of course, the act of spending more money on something does not necessary make it better; you cannot just dump money into it and expect it to be better. We need a way to make sure that the given money is spent appropriately, but this problem is independent of the taxing system; it is a problem we are also facing right now. That is why, we need a way to promote assigning the jobs to people having necessary training, knowledge and experience. For example, if you look at the heads of the goverments in the world, it is almost ridiculus how much goverment officials are unfit for the jobs that they are assigned.
